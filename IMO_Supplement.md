# IMO Consortium Supplement Rankings and Stack Recommendations


Author: ChatGPT 5.4/thinking-extended

## Scope

This document summarises the supplement comparison we built for the **Christensenella minuta + Methanobrevibacter smithii** model, where the working assumption is that the relevant structure is less like a classic thick surface biofilm and more like a **syntrophic mixed-species microaggregate / floc**. In that model, the practical targets are:

1. **Disrupt proximity / aggregate structure**
2. **Reduce hydrogen-producing bacterial pressure**
3. **Reduce methanogen pressure**
4. **Do this without worsening constipation**

## Important framing

These rankings are **mechanistic** rather than proven human head-to-head outcomes for IMO. They are intended as a decision aid for stack design, not a claim that any one product is proven to eradicate methanogen-driven constipation.

A key implication of the consortium model is that a product can be useful even if it is **not** a classic “biofilm buster” in the popular sense:

* A **disrupter** may make the aggregate more vulnerable.
* A **hydrogen-producer suppressant** may lower substrate flow to methanogens.
* A **methanogen-targeting agent** may reduce methane pressure more directly.
* For constipation-predominant IMO, **motility / clearance still matters**, otherwise disruption can underperform.

## Practical interpretation for constipation

In this use case, constipation changes the ranking logic.

A supplement can be “good on paper” yet still feel bad in practice if it:

* disrupts aggregates but does not improve clearance,
* reduces bacteria while slowing stool further,
* or increases symptom noise without improving transit.

### Practical constipation principles

* **Disruption alone is rarely enough** in methane constipation.
* **H₂-side suppression matters** because it can reduce substrate flow to methanogens.
* **Methane-side pressure matters** because methane itself is linked to slowed transit.
* **Motility / clearance still matters**, otherwise disrupted material may simply linger.
* **Bismuth-containing products deserve caution** if you already tend toward stool slowing.
* **Sulfur-heavy products deserve caution** if they create symptom noise that makes it hard to judge whether the stack is helping.

## Bottom line

### Best by category

* **Best disrupter:** Biota Dissolve
* **Best H₂-producer suppressant:** Candibactin-BR
* **Best methanogen-directed agent:** Allimax Pro
* **Best methane-constipation alternative to allicin:** Atrantil
* **Best essential-oil methane alternative:** Candibactin-AR / ADP Oregano

### My overall view

If the working model is correct, the most rational stack design is:

**disrupt proximity -> suppress hydrogen producers -> pressure methanogens -> maintain clearance**

That is more likely to work than thinking in terms of a single “biofilm buster” solving the whole problem.
